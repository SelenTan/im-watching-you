My cat is an ordinary cat. Sometimes he is clever: he knows his name, and he absolutely knows how to find a human when dinner is late.

But he is also a foolish little gentleman, because he has never understood one important fact: I am not another cat.

This misunderstanding has consequences. He wants to play with me the way cats play with each other, which means chasing, pouncing, biting, and treating my ankles as moving prey.

That sounds sweet in theory. Your cat wants to play with you. How adorable.

In real life, it feels more like living with a tiny orange assassin. He hides under the sofa, on top of the fridge, inside blankets, under tables, and in every shadowy place where human eyes cannot reach. Then he waits.

There are four people in my family: my dad, my mom, my sister, and me. Somehow, he only ambushes me.

Every summer when I go home, I stop wearing shorts indoors. It can be 36 ℃ outside, but I still choose long trousers, because nobody can trim Tian Ju's nails, and nobody's skin is strong enough to survive his enthusiastic little attacks.

If I do not want my legs covered in scratches, I simply accept my fate.

One day, I hope he discovers peace. Until then, I will continue living under orange surveillance.
